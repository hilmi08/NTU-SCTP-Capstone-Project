# Starbucks Corporation — Financial & Strategic Analytics Capstone

A comprehensive data analytics capstone project examining Starbucks Corporation's
financial trajectory from FY2018 to FY2025, with forward-looking revenue forecasts
for FY2026 and strategic scenario recommendations.

## 📌 Project Overview

This project was developed as a team capstone, covering three analytical acts:

1. **Time Series Analysis** — Quarterly revenue trends across markets (North America,
   International, Channel Development), store types, and lines of business (Beverage,
   Food, Other), benchmarked against direct and indirect competitors.

2. **Revenue Forecasting** — Multi-method forecasting using 3-MA, 5-MA Symmetric,
   Exponential Smoothing (ES), and Excel's FORECAST.ETS function, producing FY2026
   estimates across three scenarios (Expected: ~$39.6B, Optimistic: ~$41.4B,
   Pessimistic: ~$37.7B).

3. **Scenario Planning & Strategic Recommendations** — PEST and SWOT analysis with
   tailored playbooks for Steady Turnaround, Rapid Growth, and Macro Headwinds
   scenarios, covering Starbucks' "Back to Starbucks" strategy.

## 🛠️ Tools & Methods

- **Excel** — Data modeling, time series smoothing, FORECAST.ETS, scenario tables
- **PowerPoint** — Executive presentation and data storytelling
- **Forecasting Methods** — 3-MA Simple, 5-MA Symmetric, Exponential Smoothing (α=0.3, α=0.7), ETS with confidence bounds
- **Frameworks** — PEST Analysis, SWOT Analysis, Scenario Planning

## 🏢 Companies Analyzed

- **Primary:** Starbucks Corporation (SBUX)
- **Direct Competitors:** Luckin Coffee, Tim Hortons (RBI), Dutch Bros
- **Indirect Competitors:** McDonald's, Restaurant Brands International, Yum! Brands

## 📈 Key Findings

- North America's revenue share grew from 68% (FY2018) to 74% (FY2025),
  creating concentration risk
- Asset-light revenue streams (royalties, Channel Development) outperform
  traditional company-operated stores
- Luckin Coffee poses the most credible competitive threat in China
- FY2025 net earnings decline of 62% YoY reflects deliberate restructuring,
  not structural collapse
- FY2026 Expected Scenario: ~$39.6B revenue (+6.5% YoY)
